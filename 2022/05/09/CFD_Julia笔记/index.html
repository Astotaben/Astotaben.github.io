1.3 Crank-Nicolson Scheme

second-order accurate in time numerical scheme

combining forward in time method at (n) and the backward in time method at (n+1)

implicit numerical schemes. —solved by inverting the matrix

The main advantage of an implicit numerical scheme is that they are unconditionally stable.

This allows the use of larger time step and grid size without affecting the convergence of the numerical scheme.

ui(n)=ui(n+1)−∂ui(n+1)∂tΔt+O(Δt2),(15)\large u^{(n)}_i = u^{(n+1)}_i - \frac{\partial u^{(n+1)}_i}{\partial t}\Delta t + \Omicron (\Delta t^2), \tag {15} ui(n)​=ui(n+1)​−∂t∂ui(n+1)​​Δt+O(Δt2),(15)

∂ui(n+1)∂t=ui(n+1)−ui(n)Δt+O(Δt).(16)\large \frac{\partial u^{(n+1)}_i}{\partial t} = \frac{u^{(n+1)}_i - u^{(n)}_i}{\Delta t}+\Omicron (\Delta t). \tag {16} ∂t∂ui(n+1)​​=Δtui(n+1)​−ui(n)​​+O(Δt).(16)

∂2ui(n+1)∂x2=ui+1(n+1)−2ui(n+1)+ui−1(n+1)Δx2+O(Δx2).(17)\large \frac{\partial^2 u^{(n+1)}_i}{\partial x^2} = \frac{u^{(n+1)}_{i+1}-2u^{(n+1)}_i+u^{(n+1)}_{i-1}}{\Delta x^2} + \Omicron (\Delta x^2). \tag {17} ∂x2∂2ui(n+1)​​=Δx2ui+1(n+1)​−2ui(n+1)​+ui−1(n+1)​​+O(Δx2).(17)

1.4 Implicit Compact Pade (ICP) Scheme

Measuring the order of accuracy is the modified wavenumber approach

The modified wavenumber varies for every finite difference scheme and can be found using Fourier analysis of the differencing scheme

Compact finite difference schemes have very good resolution characteristics and can be used for
capturing high-frequency waves . In compact formulation, we express the derivative of a function
as a linear combination of values of function defined on a set of nodes.

  • The fourth-order accurate approximation to the second derivative

112∂2u∂x2∣i−1+1012∂2u∂x2∣i+112∂2u∂x2∣i+1=ui+1−2ui+ui−1Δx2(21)\large \frac{1}{12}\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2}\mid _{i-1} + \frac{10}{12}\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2}\mid _{i} + \frac{1}{12}\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2}\mid _{i+1} =\frac{u_{i+1}-2u_i+u_{i-1}}{\Delta x^2} \tag{21} 121​∂x2∂2u​∣i−1​+1210​∂x2∂2u​∣i​+121​∂x2∂2u​∣i+1​=Δx2ui+1​−2ui​+ui−1​​(21)
